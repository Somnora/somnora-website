Founder's Note

Your life is your life.

Your dreams, memories, reflections, ideas, and motivations belong to you. They are not something to be extracted, mined, or fed into targeted advertising and data broker systems. We will never sell your data or quietly turn your private journaling into ad-targeting inventory. Some Somnora features do send content to our services so Nora can respond, speak, or generate imagery, but that should feel like product function, not hidden exploitation. If you choose to share feedback on individual responses, that choice is yours.

I made Somnora to distract myself from what I now know is PTSD, so I always had someone around. Trusting a company with your inner life should not mean watching that trust get packaged, mined, and sold. That is gross. It is a betrayal. And it is not who we are.

Common Questions

Is this therapy?
No. Somnora is a private companion for sleep and reflection, not therapy or medical care. It helps you capture what lingers and revisit it with context.
Do I need to remember full dreams?
Not at all. A feeling, image, sentence, or strange detail is enough. Somnora is built for fragments as much as full stories.
What does HealthKit add?
It gives Nora sleep duration, stages, and timing context, so your reflections sit beside something measurable instead of floating on their own.
Can I export my reflections?
Yes. Somnora lets you export available data from the app, and you can clear local data from Settings.
How does Nora adjust its tone?
Nora adapts between reflective, structured, and calm modes depending on context, while staying short-first and non-clinical.
