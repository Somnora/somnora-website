Common Questions

Is this a replacement for therapy?
No, and it doesn't try to be. Somnora is not a replacement for therapy or medical care, but it can be a genuinely useful tool alongside it: the patterns Nora helps you notice (recurring themes, sleep context, what keeps returning) make rich, concrete context to bring into conversations with your therapist, or anyone helping you take care of yourself.
How often does Nora reach out?
At most once a day, and only when she actually noticed something worth your attention: a follow-up on something you mentioned, a pattern that's forming, a rough stretch of sleep. If nothing has earned it, she stays quiet. Silence is a feature.
Do I need to remember full dreams?
Not at all. A feeling, image, sentence, or strange detail is enough. Somnora is built for fragments as much as full stories.
What does Apple Health add?
Sleep duration, stages, and timing context, so your reflections sit beside something measurable instead of floating on their own. It's always an explicit opt-in, and Nora describes trends in plain language, never scores.
Does Nora have a voice?
Yes. Morning capture is voice-first: you can talk before you're fully awake and Nora transcribes as you speak. She can also read wind-downs and meditations aloud in a warm, natural voice. And you can always just type.
Can I export my reflections?
Yes. Your entries export from the app whenever you want them, including a clean, therapist-ready summary if you'd like to bring your patterns into a session. Local data can be cleared from Settings anytime.
How does Nora adjust her tone?
Nora adapts between reflective, structured, and calm modes depending on context, while staying short-first and non-clinical.
